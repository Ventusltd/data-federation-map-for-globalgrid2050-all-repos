# The Spider's Sensory System

Provenance: declared Spider DNA doctrine. Written 2 July 2026. Status: vocabulary and boundary doctrine only. This file applies no data, changes no workflow, alters no UI, generates no cartridge, adds no source, and certifies no engineering result.

## Doctrine

The Spider does not only map repositories.

A repository is one kind of node, not the whole world.

The Spider may also map eyes, senses, sources and surfaces.

An eye is a geospatial rendering app.

A sense is a time-series or market-intelligence app.

A source is an external API, portal or dataset provider.

A surface is a served live page that a human can open.

## Relationship Rules

A repository may host an eye or a sense.

A surface may render an eye or a sense.

An eye may expose cartridges.

A sense may expose pipelines.

A source may feed many eyes and senses.

A degraded surface is still truth.

A broken live page must be shown as degraded, not hidden by the existence of its repository.

## Provenance Rules

The Spider must read declared local manifests before it infers from code.

The Spider may chart non-repository things only when their provenance is explicit.

Declared means the app, repo or principal states it.

Derived means the scanner inferred it.

Derived must never impersonate declared.

A source declared in a manifest is declared.

A source found by code scan is derived until accepted by human dispatch.

AI may propose target registry entries.

Only principal dispatch may apply them.

## Boundary

The Spider may smell, see and chart.

The Spider may not certify.

All joined grid, market and geospatial outputs remain screening-grade until a qualified professional accepts responsibility.

The Spider may point at patterns, degraded surfaces, missing manifests, candidate sources and candidate joins.

The Spider must not present screening output as a connection offer, design certification, operational truth or professional engineering advice.
