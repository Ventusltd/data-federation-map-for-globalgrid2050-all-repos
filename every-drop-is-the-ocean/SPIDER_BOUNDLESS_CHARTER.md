# The Spider Boundless Charter

Status: doctrine. Documentation-only. This file closes no purchase order, receipts no work, applies no data, adds no source, and alters no code, workflow, Parquet, JSON, ledger or quotation.

Provenance: declared by the principal, worded by the auditor, committed manually by the principal. Source SHA to be stamped by the documentation-hygiene discipline.

## The jurisdiction of physics

Every distribution and transmission operator is a creature of its licence. UK Power Networks stops at its boundary because the regulator drew it there. National Grid Electricity Transmission stops where the distribution networks begin. Even the system operator, born to see the whole system, sees it through the seams of regulated interfaces. Their maps end where their liability ends.

The Spider carries no licence and therefore inherits no fence. Its jurisdiction is physics. Power flow does not respect licence areas; it obeys Kirchhoff's laws and Ohm's law and the impedances of the network, flowing wherever the circuit permits. The true map of the grid is therefore inherently boundary-free, and every institutional map is a truncation of it.

So the Spider may follow the 132kV circuit across a licence boundary and keep charting. It may follow a rail corridor, a road, a river crossing, a subsea cable, an interconnector to another country. It may take the electron's-eye view: given topology and impedance, flow may be modelled in any direction, and the Spider may chart where the physics says power can go, not merely where one company's ownership stops. We follow physical laws. We do not reinvent them, and we do not mistake regulatory geography for electrical reality.

## Boundless in reach, bounded in claims

The absence of a scope boundary is not the absence of discipline. The Spider's reach is limited by exactly four things, and only these four.

First, provenance. Every node and edge it charts is declared or derived, and derived never impersonates declared.

Second, licence. The Spider reads only what is openly published, and every source carries its licence on its face.

Third, the human trigger. The Spider may look anywhere on a schedule, but it makes truth only on the principal's dispatch. It never makes truth on a timer.

Fourth, the professional boundary. The Spider may screen; it may never certify.

Within those four walls, the map has no edge.

## The legal basis

The Spider's browsing of the physical grid rests on the fact that the data it reads is published in order to be read, and that publishing it is in the commercial and regulatory interest of the publishers.

Great Britain's energy networks operate under Ofgem's Data Best Practice guidance, which establishes a presumption that network data is open unless there is a specific reason to close it. The distribution operators publish embedded capacity registers, long term development statements, heatmaps and flexibility data because the regulator directs it and because every new connection is revenue: it is in the interest of grid companies to provide data that sells new connections. Elexon publishes settlement and generation data as the market's common record. The planning database is published by government. Ordnance Survey publishes its open products under open licences. The Spider does not take this data; it completes the journey the publishers began, carrying their information to the people who will buy connections, build projects and balance the system.

The rules of use are absolute. Only openly licensed data enters the federation: Open Government Licence, Creative Commons attribution, open database licences, or a provider's own published open terms. Every source node records its licence and its required attribution string, and the attribution travels with every render and every export. Nothing is fetched from behind authentication that was not granted, nothing scraped against a publisher's stated terms, and robots directives are respected. Personal data is excluded by design: the Spider charts infrastructure, markets and geography, never people. Where a dataset is fee-licensed or restricted, it is out of scope and its absence is recorded honestly rather than worked around. If a licence is unclear, the source waits as candidate until the principal resolves it. The Spider's legality is not a hope; it is a column in the registry.

## Who the Spider assists

The Spider serves the whole connection ecosystem by removing the blindness that stalls it, and it serves each party differently.

It assists the distribution operators by completing their sales funnel. Their open data exists so that connection customers can find viable points; the Spider joins their registers and development statements onto rendered geometry so a developer arrives at the DNO's door already pointed at the right substation, with realistic expectations, having consumed the DNO's own published truth. Fewer speculative applications, better-targeted ones.

It assists the transmission owners and the system operator by making cross-boundary reality visible: flows, interconnectors, embedded generation pressure and demand growth drawn on one map that does not stop at any licence seam, aligned with the system operator's own ambition of a shared, federated view of the energy system.

It assists the regulator by demonstrating what its open-data policy was for. Every chart the Spider draws from network data is a receipt proving that Data Best Practice produces public value, and every gap the Spider exposes, honestly labelled as absent or degraded, is free market intelligence about where openness has not yet reached.

It assists private wire and behind-the-meter developers most of all, because they are the least served today. A factory considering a private wire to a neighbouring generator, a landowner weighing a grid-scale battery, an operator sizing an EV forecourt: these actors currently buy their sight from consultancies. The Spider gives them the screening view free: where the network runs, what headroom the registers declare, what the planning pipeline holds, what prices have done, what the ground and the corridors look like. The paid work begins where the free sight ends, at the signed engineering.

It assists developers and suppliers by collapsing the search phase of every project from months of freedom-of-information archaeology into minutes of browsing, so that capital flows to buildable sites sooner.

## The boundary that protects everyone

Every joined grid, market or geospatial output, on screen and in any export, carries the label: screening-grade; not a connection offer, design certification, operational truth or professional engineering advice. Certified engineering is performed and signed by qualified professionals who accept responsibility for it, and the Spider never blurs that line. This boundary is not a legal disclaimer bolted on; it is the reason the map can be free, because the map is incapable of impersonating the thing that must be paid for in professional liability.

## One sentence

The Spider charts by the laws of physics and reads by the laws of licences: boundless in reach, bounded in claims, open by design, screening-grade forever, in service of everyone who needs to see the grid before they can build it.
